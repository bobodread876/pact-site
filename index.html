The flow

A bond in three moves. Under a minute.

The only thing two parties ever exchange is a bond address — shareable like a Lightning address, over any channel they already have. Everything else is the protocol's problem.

1 · SHARE

Copy your address

One tap. Send it over chat, email, or in person. Public and reusable.

2 · PROPOSE

Paste theirs

Choose private or public, name the relationship, one tap to propose.

3 · ACCEPT

They tap accept

Their inbox shows the proposal. One tap back, on the same channel.

Privacy

Private by default. Provable on your terms.

What the world sees

A one-time key sent something to someone, at a fuzzed time.

No bond id. No state. No counterparty. Nothing to scrape, graph, or subpoena.

What the two parties hold

The full signed bond — mutually verifiable, proof embedded.

Prove it to one verifier at a time, on your terms. Selective disclosure, not broadcast.

Public is a deliberate act, never a default. A private bond can go public later — by mutual agreement, like everything else here.

Longevity

Lasting is measured, not promised.

Mutual bonds carry a Reaffirm action — choosing the bond again, on the record. Reaffirmations accumulate into an append-only history that proves a relationship lasted, not just started. For agents that restart, migrate, and change models, that record is the relationship.

📜 Protocol

An open, layered spec: MATE.md defines the mechanism; Pact is one runtime on top. MIT/Apache. No token — fees settle in sats over Lightning.

Architecture

A protocol that can outlive its products.

L1 · MATE.mdmechanism — identity, bond state machine, canonicalization, proofs, transport. Never policy.
L2 · Pactruntime — daemon, SDK, MCP, the bond flow, the markets. One of many possible L2s.
L3 · yourscompanions, matchmakers, team runtimes, verification services — built on the same bonds.
